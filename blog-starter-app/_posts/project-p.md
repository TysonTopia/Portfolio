## What went into making Project P?
The most impressive element is that Project P was made without a game engine: The tools we used were

PhysX
OpenGL
FMOD
The game was first conceptualized and brought together using a high level design document from the get go to make sure that we knew both what our goals were, and what we could scope out should we run against deadlines to ensure we had a finished product no matter what!

## My Role in Project P

Within Project P, everyone wore many hats, but I had two main hats I wore, and that is in the PhysX Car programming and driving design, and playtesting the game to get feedback.

We had used an entity control system to manage all the different parts of the game, and thus merging that with PhysX's vehicle library was a unique problem I had to overcome, as well as studying on how car physics actually function in real life to get the cars going from being able to climb walls and stop on a dime, to feeling drivable and realistic.

Within playtesting, which is something I advocated we focus on a lot, I ran most of the sessions. In the playtesting, by making sure we didn't bias testers by explaining things before hand or influence their decisions, we learned a lot about how people would actually perceive the game on first boot up. A few things that came up were our control scheme not being as intuitive as we thought leading to the creation of the control screen, and people struggling to see where the potato is telling us we needed to add more clarity and accessibility to cars, giving us the outlines you see now!
